Electricity Creation Facts


Definition of a Killowatt Hour

KilloWatt Hour is a measure of electrical energy equivalent to a power consumption of 1,000 watts for 1 hour.

Average Household Usage

The average household uses 8.5 - 10 KilloWatt Hours a day
About 62.8% of the electricity generated in the U.S. is from fossil fuel

Calculations

To find how much carbon emissions are created from average we get the average amount of Coal, Natural Gas, and Petroleum created energy used.

Natural Gas creates about 3.21 KilloWatt Hours
Coal creates about 2.99 KilloWatt Hours
Petroleum creates about 0.08 KilloWatt Hours

Multiply the Killowatt Hours with Amount of CO2 created

Natural Gas 3.21 * 0.00018 = 0.0005778 tCO2e
Coal 2.99 * 0.00036 = 0.0010764 tCO2e
Petroleum 0.08 * 0.00023 = 0.0000184 tCO2e
